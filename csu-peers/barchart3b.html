National Peers Chart Explanations

What data am I looking at?

The historical data in these charts are downloaded from the College Results Online (CRO) website (collegeresults.org). They reflect 4 and 6 year graduation rates as reported by each institution to the Integrated Postsecondary Education Data System (IPEDS), which is the federal government's annual survey of higher education institutions.

  • The CSU Peers Comparison bar chart shows the selected campus and all of its CRO identified peers.
  • The default view for the Historical Trends chart shows the selected campus and its top four institutional peers as measured by its most recent year's (4-year or 6-year) graduation rate. Note that the years shown in the x-axis reflect the year that the students entered as freshmen. If, for example, you select "6-Year Graduation Rates" the data points for the year 2008 are for students who began as first-time, full-time freshmen in the fall of 2008 and graduated by or before the end of summer 2014.

How were the peer campuses selected?

College Results Online (CRO), which is a web tool provided by the Education Trust (edtrust.org), uses 12 variables that they have found to significantly correlate to 6-year graduation rates, in order to identify a small set of institutional peers from a data set of over 1,450 institutions. They collect their data from multiple sources including the Integrated Postsecondary Education Data System (IPEDS), other Department of Education databases; Barron's Profile of American Colleges; College Board; Peterson's Databases; and the National Association of State Student Grant and Aid Programs. Details on their methodology can be found under the Methodology tab on this page. Further details can be found here: http://collegeresults.org/aboutthedata.aspx#section-5.

How do I interact with this page?

CSU Peer Comparisons bar chart:

At the top of the page, use the drop-down menus to choose the type of graduation rate you'd like to see (4-year or 6-year graduation rates), the CSU campus that you want to compare to its national peers, and the entering freshmen cohort year. If, for example, you select "6-Year Graduation Rates" and "2008" as your entering freshmen cohort, this corresponds to students who started as first-time, full-time freshmen in the fall of 2008 and graduated by or before the end of summer 2014.

Historical Trends line chart:

At the top of the page, use the drop-down menus to choose the type of graduation rate you'd like to see (4-year or 6-year graduation rates), the CSU campus that you want to compare to its national peers, and the timespan over which you'd like to view the outcome trend data.

  • Three choices are offered for timespan selections "Since 2000", shows graduation rates for freshmen entering the selected CSU campus as far back as the year 2000. The other time span options, "Past Five Years" and "Past Three Years", shows graduation rates for freshmen entering in the most recent 5 years (or 3 years) for which we have data.
  • In the figure legend, click on any campus to hide or show its trend line in the chart above.

Both Charts

  • Hover over any bar in the bar chart, or data point in the line graph, to see the institution's graduation rate (if using a smart phone or tablet, tap a bar or data point to see its data value).

Also note:

  • The latest publicly available four-year graduation rate data for peer institutions is shown for the 2008 cohort graduating in 2012. For CSU campuses, more current data is available, including the 2009, 2010, and 2011 cohorts.

National Peers Methodology

Identifying Peer Campuses

The national peer institutions selected for this dashboard come from College Results Online (CRO). CRO applies an algorithm comparing the chosen school to all other public and private not-for-profit institutions in the CRO database. Each comparison receives a "similarity score", ranging from 0 (least similar) to 1000 (identical), based on how similar the two schools are in terms of 12 institutional and student characteristics. The 12 selected characteristics are statistically correlated with overall six-year graduation rates for institutions in the public and private not-for-profit sectors (see Table 1). Once the baseline similarity score is calculated, CRO applies additional "filters" to exclude an institution from possible comparison if it greatly deviates from the chosen institution on any one of a number of factors (see CRO Website for list of filters). This prevents, for example, an institution with 2,500 undergraduates from being compared to an institution with 25,000 undergraduates, even if they are otherwise very similar.

Average Annual Change

The average annual improvement rates reported in the table to the right of the Historical Trends line chart, were calculated using the formula below:

G2 — G1⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯Y2 — Y1

where G2 is the most recent year's graduation rate, G1 is the graduation rate of the earliest year in the selected time frame, Y2 is the most recent year, and Y1 is the earliest year in the selected time frame.
